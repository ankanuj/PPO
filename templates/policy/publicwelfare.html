Eradication of Poverty, Social Welfare Policies in India: Initiatives and Policy Implications

India is essentially a nation of villages. It is predominantly an agricultural country where about 70 percent of the people are dependent upon land for their livelihood and 75 per cent live in rural India. Indian villages are suffering from economic distress due to the lower per capita income, illiteracy and wide spread malnutrition and under nutrition. They also suffer from the absence of basic amenities such as better housing facilities, transportation and communication services. In view of the recommendations of the commission appointed from time to time in accordance to the policies and programmes implemented from government and other agencies for socio-economic development of rural people the paper explores government initiatives, implications and explores the connotations of development of rural people.
